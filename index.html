Ghost of Yōtei

In the snow-covered lands of northern Japan, where the mountains rise like ancient guardians and silence holds its breath between the trees, a warrior named Atsu walks a path carved by loss. Sixteen years ago, her family was torn apart by a ruthless group known as the Yōtei Six. She survived the massacre as a child, carrying the memory of fire and steel in her mind. From that moment, she vowed to return. Not as a victim, but as a blade reborn.
Now grown, Atsu has become an onna-musha, a warrior trained in the arts of stealth, swordsmanship, and survival. As her revenge unfolds, Atsu begins to uncover a deeper truth. Vengeance can consume more than it frees, and even the strongest warrior must choose what kind of ghost she wishes to become.
Her story isn’t only about blood or battle. It’s about the line between honor and fury, about finding light in a world shadowed by war. Beneath the northern stars, Atsu’s legend grows, giving us a reminder that even in the coldest places, fire can still burn bright.
